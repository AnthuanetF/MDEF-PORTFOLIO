//Designing in a
State of Climate Emergency.

The context in which we design 
In the 21st century.


 

In the time I find myself in, there are various visible and invisible frictions in social,economic, political, environmental, and revolutionary realms. It is key to allow ourselves to observe our relationship with our space and time in order to design new transformative realities. I share reflections and explorations, these starting by understanding what Polycrisis is.

 A singular crisis made up of many crises.

Polycrisis

Poly-crisis 
(Non lineal)

In this complex global landscape, we face a series of challenges that demand a comprehensive and collaborative response. The pandemic, conflicts, extreme weather events, and more call us to rethink and act with determination. However, amid the complexity, there arises an opportunity to embrace a more creative and collaborative approach. The "polycrisis" challenges us to find integrated solutions where innovation and adaptability are paramount. By adopting a holistic perspective, we can discover new ways to address these interconnected challenges.

Solar Center Design

Solar center design invites us to explore meanings and challenge established norms, collectively connecting to the Sun, as my colleagues and I did, recalling our unique relationship with the Earth. It seeks healing through ancestral remembrance, taking us back to the primitive: the sun, the fire, the campfire.

This approach suggests a form of design that transcends mere efficiency to immerse itself in the essential connection with nature. It is a call to feel part of something greater, cultivating a design vision that reflects our sacred connection with the Earth and the Sun from within, acknowledging the power of collectivity. This ritualistic exercise of feeling and thinking represents a "hack" to our logical minds, accustomed to receiving binary information and responding linearly.
